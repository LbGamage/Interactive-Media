—•‧₊˚⊹ HUMAN COMPUTER IN YOUR FACE •‧₊˚⊹—

In this workshop, we put together a gesture glossary by noting down different movements and behaviors that triggered interactions between people and non-human systems. We looked at how simple gestures could act as commands or signals in human-computer interaction.

Initially, our group came up with a funny hand gesture, but we misunderstood the assignment. We didn't realize the gesture had to be one that allowed a person to interact with a computer system. This was supposed to be a sort of 'double fk u'

The second interaction gesture we brainstormed involved using a meme to verify that a user is human in the form of a captcha. The user would see a meme and be required to vocalize the audio, creating a fun and unique experience. We though it could kind of be a secret code, similar to a password for a speakeasy, offering exclusive access to content for people who get the meme.

At home, I thought of a more intriguing gesture: crocheting, as it's something I do oftem. The hand movements involved in crocheting are rhythmic and repetitive, with one hand guiding the yarn and the other maneuvering the hook to form stitches. This reminded me of futuristic scenes in movies like 'Iron Man', where characters use hand gestures to manipulate technology in a 3D space
